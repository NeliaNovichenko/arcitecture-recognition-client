American Foursquare
The American Foursquare or American Four Square is an American house style popular from the mid-1890s to the late 1930s. The hallmarks of the style include a basically square, boxy design, usually with four large, boxy rooms to a floor, a center dormer, and a large front porch with wide stairs. Other common features included a hipped roof, arched entries between common rooms, built-in cabinetry, and Craftsman-style woodwork.
